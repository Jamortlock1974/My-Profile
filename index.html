A little about me

I have loved books for as long as I can remember. I waited at the gate for the postman once a month when I was five, he delivered a magic slip letting me know my next Disney adventure had arrived at the post office. When I was twelve I walked into walls because I couldn't put Agatha Christie down to join my family for supper. I also got sent to the principal's office once during art class because my Moses received the ten commandments from a UFO (an adult had carelessly left an Eric Von Daniken thesis unattended). My children had their first library cards by the time they were four months old. I don't have a favourite author, that would require multiple alphabetical lists, arranged by genre, also alphabetically. This is a very loose collection of books with such an odd architecture that they always come to mind when I'm asked by friends "any recommendations on what I should read next?"

My top 5: magical realist metafiction with a capital M!

  1. Piranesi by Susanna Clark
  2. House of Leaves by Mark Z. Danielewski
  3. Life of Pi by Yann Martel
  4. Midnight's Children by Salman Rushdie
  5. American Gods by Neil Gaiman
